The Story

Armand "Mondo" Duplantis is not just rewriting the history of pole vaulting—he’s defining what’s possible in the sport. Born in 1999 in Lafayette, Louisiana, Mondo’s connection to the pole vault was almost predestined, as he was raised in a family with deep athletic roots. His father, Greg Duplantis, was a former pole vaulter, and his mother, Helena, was a former heptathlete. Mondo's rise to greatness was swift, and by the age of 18, he was already a world-class pole vaulter, setting his sights on breaking world records.

Duplantis’ career took off when he broke the world junior pole vault record at just 16 years old. His remarkable talent was evident, but it wasn’t until he made the transition to the senior level that the world truly began to take notice. In 2020, Mondo broke the indoor pole vault world record with a jump of 6.17 meters, and just a few months later, he went even higher, reaching 6.18 meters, setting a new world record.

His journey to greatness has not been without its challenges. While other athletes faced fierce competition in their respective sports, Mondo’s only rival seemed to be gravity itself. With each competition, he defied expectations and pushed the boundaries of what was thought possible in pole vaulting. His performances have redefined the limits of the sport, and his ability to remain calm and focused, even under pressure, is a testament to his incredible mental fortitude.

Mondo’s Olympic debut at the Tokyo 2020 Games (held in 2021) saw him take home a gold medal, proving he could handle the biggest stage in the world. The victory was another milestone in his already impressive career, and it marked the beginning of a new era in pole vaulting. His combination of raw talent, technical skill, and determination has made him one of the most exciting athletes to watch in the world of track and field.

What sets Mondo apart is not just his record-breaking jumps but his humble demeanor and constant desire to improve. He trains relentlessly, driven by the love of the sport and the thrill of breaking barriers. Mondo Duplantis' story is one of relentless pursuit, a reminder that no matter the challenge, greatness is possible with the right mindset and dedication.

From a young talent to the world record-holder, Mondo’s journey continues to inspire athletes and fans alike, proving that the sky is the limit—literally.

Achievements

  • World Record Holder – Holds the outdoor pole vault world record at 6.18 meters, set in 2020, and the indoor pole vault world record at 6.17 meters, set in 2020.
  • 2020 Olympic Gold Medalist – Won the gold medal in pole vaulting at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics (held in 2021), cementing his place as one of the sport’s greats.
  • Multiple World Championship Medals – Has earned multiple medals at the World Athletics Championships, including gold in 2019.
  • Youngest Ever World Record Holder – Became the youngest person to break the pole vault world record at 20 years old.
  • European Champion – Won gold at the European Athletics Championships, further proving his dominance in the sport.
  • Indoor and Outdoor World Record Holder – Owns both the indoor and outdoor world records, showcasing his versatility across different environments.
  • World Junior Record Holder – Set the world junior pole vault record at 16 years old, signaling his extraordinary potential from a young age.
  • Numerous International Titles – Has claimed victories in several prestigious international competitions, including the Diamond League and Continental Cup.
  • Named IAAF World Athlete of the Year – Recognized as one of the top athletes in track and field for his groundbreaking achievements.
  • Sportsmanship and Dedication – Known for his humble attitude, relentless work ethic, and sportsmanship, Mondo continues to inspire the next generation of athletes.
